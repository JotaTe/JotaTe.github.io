Home
 

I recently graduated from the GIS Certificate Program at the Univiersity of Wisconsin - Madison. Prior to my GIS studies I worked in all things Information Technology. Most recently in the technology industry I worked as a front-end web developer honing my scripting, HTML and CSS skills. Why go back to school? Why the career change? Maps. And technology.

I like maps. I like technology. I like how things are spatially related. I like how informatoin is represented on maps and the stories it can tell. When I saw my first Madison B-Cycle station in Madison, WI, I instantly thought to myself “Why did they put it there as opposed to another location?” I began to examine the area taking note of adjacent commercial properties, residential sites, bicycle paths, and other points of interest. I viewed a map of B-Cycle stations and assembled the spatial relationships to get a basic understanding of why the stations were located where they were. Technical solutions are one aspect of the greater problem, but how to visually represent these solutions and information is another. For me it is not enough to know why; I also want to be able to have the solutions and data visualized.

You can view some of my past and present work on my portfolio page. If you have any questions feel free to contact me.
